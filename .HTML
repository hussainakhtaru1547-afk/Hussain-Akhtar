Islamic Values

Featured Article: The Five Pillars of Islam

The Five Pillars of Islam are the foundation of a Muslim's faith and practice. They represent the core beliefs and duties that every Muslim must fulfill in their lifetime. These pillars include the declaration of faith (Shahada), prayer (Salat), almsgiving (Zakat), fasting during Ramadan (Sawm), and pilgrimage to Mecca (Hajj) for those who are able.

Each pillar serves to strengthen the bond between the individual and Allah, as well as foster community and social responsibility. By adhering to these pillars, Muslims demonstrate their commitment to their faith and their submission to the will of Allah.
